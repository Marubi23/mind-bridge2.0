Need Immediate Help?

You are not alone. Help is available right now, 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

If you or someone you know is in immediate danger, call 999 or go to your nearest hospital emergency room.

What to Do in a Crisis

01

Call a Helpline

Reach out to any of the numbers above. They are free, confidential, and available 24/7.

02

Stay With Someone

Don't be alone. Reach out to a trusted friend, family member, or neighbor.

03

Go to Emergency Room

If you feel unsafe, go to the nearest hospital emergency department.

04

Remove Access

If you're having thoughts of self-harm, remove any means of harm from your environment.

While You Wait for Help

Deep Breathing

Inhale for 4 seconds, hold for 4, exhale for 4. Repeat 5 times.

Splash Cold Water

Splash cold water on your face to activate your body's calming reflex.

Change Your Environment

Step outside, walk around, or move to a different room.

Ground Yourself

Name 5 things you see, 4 you can touch, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, 1 you taste.

When to Seek Immediate Help

Talking about wanting to die or kill oneself

Looking for ways to kill oneself

Talking about feeling hopeless or having no reason to live

Giving away prized possessions

Withdrawing from friends and family

Sudden calmness after severe depression

If you notice any of these signs in yourself or someone else, reach out for help immediately.
